§ 03 · Signal Contamination

Curve signals at daily frequency are contaminated.

All four curve factors show lag-0 IC three to four times higher than lag-1. The interpolated curve moves with the same prices you are trying to predict. Once you lag signals properly, the alpha disappears. Most backtests in the literature rebalance monthly and never see this.

Infrastructure finding If you run carry signals at daily frequency without proper lagging, your backtest is wrong. I built the diagnostic that catches this and documented the decay across all four curve factors.

§ 04 · Curve Structure

Reading the curve like a physical trader.

I extract convenience yield from a cost-of-carry model and classify each commodity into five regimes: deep contango through crisis backwardation. Each regime maps directly to a physical market state. This is how commodity desks actually think about curves.

Theory of Storage Convenience yield reflects physical scarcity. Low inventories push the spot premium up and the curve inverts into backwardation. This is the oldest signal in commodity markets.

§ 05 · TSI Strategy

Three layers, one thesis.

I built a three-layer strategy around the term structure: directional regime tilt (40% risk budget), transition momentum (25%), and structural spreads (35%). Every signal is grounded in physical commodity logic, not data-mined patterns.

§ 06 · What Works

The risk premium is the trade.

Equal-weight long across nineteen markets. OOS Sharpe +0.35 with zero free parameters. Hedgers pay a structural premium to transfer price risk. That premium is persistent, diversified across sectors, and survives any cost model. Every factor strategy just adds noise on top.

Zero parameters No lookback windows. No thresholds. No regime classification. No estimation error. This is the null hypothesis every commodity factor paper should benchmark against. Most of them can't beat it.
