§ 03 · Contamination

The contamination no one talks about.

All four curve factors have lag-0 information coefficients three to four times larger than lag-1. Daily curve signals are tautological — they predict returns that already happened. Academic papers use monthly rebalancing, which masks this effect.

Why it matters Academic papers using monthly rebalancing never see this. Daily rebalancing exposes the contamination — and forces you to lag signals properly, which kills the alpha.
Information coefficient at lag 0 vs lag 1 for each curve factor. The sharp decay reveals that most apparent predictive power comes from contemporaneous price correlation, not genuine forecasting ability.

§ 04 · Curve Structure

Reading the curve like a physical trader.

Convenience yield extracted from a cost-of-carry model, classified into five regimes: deep contango, mild contango, flat, mild backwardation, deep backwardation. Each regime maps to a physical market story.

Theory of Storage Convenience yield reflects physical scarcity. When inventories are low, the spot premium rises — the curve inverts into backwardation. This is the oldest signal in commodity markets.

§ 05 · TSI Strategy

Three layers, one thesis.

Directional regime tilt (40% risk), transition momentum (25%), structural spreads (35%). Each layer captures a different aspect of term structure dynamics.

§ 06 · What Works

The commodity risk premium is the alpha.

Equal-weight long-only across nineteen markets. In-sample Sharpe +0.22, out-of-sample +0.35. No active strategy beats passive long after costs. The structural risk premium — insurance sellers earn a premium from hedgers — is the only durable source of return.

Why simplicity wins Fewer parameters means less overfitting. The EW long portfolio has zero free parameters — no lookback windows, no thresholds, no regime classification. It just harvests the commodity risk premium.
Three-year rolling Sharpe ratio. EW long (gold) is consistently positive. Active factor strategies oscillate around zero with high variance.
Bootstrap 95% confidence intervals for the Sharpe ratio. Only EW long has a CI that excludes zero. All active strategies are statistically indistinguishable from noise.

§ 09 · Architecture

Nineteen markets, one pipeline.

WRDS → Curves → Factors → Signals → Backtest → Evaluation
  • Data sources · WRDS Datastream (back-month curves), yfinance (front-month, benchmarks), EIA (energy inventories), FRED (macro), CFTC (COT positioning)
  • Commodities · CL, NG, RB, HO (energy) · GC, SI, HG (metals) · ZC, ZS, ZW, KC, SB, CC (agriculture) · LE, HE, LH, LC, FC, CT (livestock & softs)
  • Factors · Carry, slope, curvature, curve momentum (structural) · TSMOM, XSMOM, CFTC positioning (behavioral) · EIA inventory surprise, macro exposure, volatility regime (fundamental)
  • Tests · 438 unit tests, ruff linting, expanding-window validation, no lookahead
